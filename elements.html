The Shortest War in History

The Anglo-Zanzibar War of 1896 holds the record for the shortest war in history. It lasted between 38 and 45 minutes! The conflict was between the United Kingdom and the Sultanate of Zanzibar. The war broke out after the death of the pro-British Sultan Hamoud bin Mohammed and the subsequent rise of a rival sultan. The British, who supported Hamoud’s succession, launched a bombardment that quickly overwhelmed the defenses of the opposing sultan, leading to a swift and decisive victory.
