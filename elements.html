Anthropocene Reviewed

ANTHROPOCENE

The Anthropocene concept, which claims that humans have become the planet's top environmental influencers, strikes me as a classic case of humans overestimating their importance. It's like saying we're the stars of Earth's reality show, despite the fact that many of us are more like background extras. This idea gets 2 stars from me—one for trying to give us a starring role in Earth's history and an extra one for its sheer audacity.

SOCIAL MEDIA

Social media: the digital playground where we pretend our lives are perfect and judge others based on their highlight reels. It's like a never-ending high school reunion, but with better filters. I give it 3 stars – one for connecting us globally, one for providing endless cat videos, and one for giving us the ability to mute that annoying relative during election season.

Couplet Explanation

This couplet, written by Daagh Dehlvi, reflects on the paradox of existence. It can be translated as: "O Daagh, if you're tired of life, why do you continue living? Life isn't dear to you, yet you don't depart from it either." The poet questions the human tendency to cling to life even when it becomes burdensome, highlighting the complex relationship between suffering and the will to live.

Couplet Explanation

This couplet by Mirza Ghalib can be translated as: "We hope for loyalty from those Who don't even know what loyalty is." Ghalib is expressing the irony and perhaps futility of expecting faithfulness or loyalty from someone who doesn't understand or value these concepts. It's a commentary on misplaced expectations in relationships and the pain that can result from them.
